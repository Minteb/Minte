Background

My Story

Growing up in Ethiopia, I witnessed how fragile agricultural systems are when confronted with environmental change. That observation became a lifelong question: can the view from space help us understand, protect, and sustainably manage the landscapes that feed us?

Over more than a decade, I have built expertise at the intersection of Earth observation, machine learning, and agricultural science — from lecturing GIS and remote sensing at Mettu and Wollega Universities, to leading research as a PhD candidate at Addis Ababa University's School of Earth Sciences. My doctoral work integrates multi-sensor satellite data (Sentinel-1 SAR, Sentinel-2, Landsat, LiDAR GEDI) with AI-driven pipelines to forecast crop yields and assess nutritional outcomes in smallholder farming landscapes.

Today I contribute to international research collaborations including the ViSTA initiative (multi-country crop mapping and yield analytics) and the CGIAR SPIA ADD-IN Ethiopia project on agricultural innovation adoption. I am equally committed to knowledge transfer — training the next generation of African spatial scientists who can turn satellite data into decisions that matter at the local level.
